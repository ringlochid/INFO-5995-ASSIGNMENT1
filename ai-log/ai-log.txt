Rubric-Driven Mock Q&A (Required)
--------------------------------
Q: What is the protected asset and attacker capability?
A: Protected assets are the username/password and `sessionToken`. The attacker is a local analyst (rooted or forensic access) who can read app-private files. This directly enables credential theft and session token recovery.

Q: What is the exact vulnerability and where is it located?
A: Plaintext credentials are written to `credentials.txt` in `MainActivity`. Session tokens are generated using `java.util.Random` in `Login.generateSessionToken()` and stored in `SharedPreferences` unencrypted.

Q: Why is this security-relevant and how could it be exploited?
A: Plaintext storage enables direct credential theft from extracted app data. `Random` is deterministic; tokens can be predicted if an attacker can bound the seed or observe timing. If tokens are later used for authorization, this enables session hijacking.

Q: What is the concrete fix?
A: Store only salted, iterated password hashes (PBKDF2) in encrypted storage; use `SecureRandom` for tokens and store them in encrypted preferences.
